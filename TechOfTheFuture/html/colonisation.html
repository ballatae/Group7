Colonisation

The Colonisation of Space



We've sent astronauts to the Moon. Is it possible to live in space? Is it possible to build and inhabit permanent colonies on the surface of the Earth? These issues would have been regarded as science fiction, fantasy, or, at best, wishful thinking by folks ahead of their time not long ago. They are now being seriously considered not merely out of human curiosity, but also because current events encourage the notion that space colonization could provide significant benefits and hope to a more contained and limited humanity. Off the Earth, permanent communities can be established and inhabited. The chapters that follow provide a detailed description of a system for space colonization. It is not the best or most complete system that can be designed.



The History of Space Exploration

During the time that has passed since the launching of the first artificial satellite in 1957, astronauts have traveled to the moon, probes have explored the solar system, and instruments in space have discovered thousands of planets around other stars.

Apollo 11 Astronauts on Moon

A less belligerent, but no less competitive, part of the Cold War between the Soviet Union and the United States was the space race. The Soviet Union bested its rival at nearly every turn, until the United States beat them to the finish line by landing astronauts on the moon. Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin completed that mission in 1969.
